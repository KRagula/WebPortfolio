BAJOH WATER PROJECT AND BANGEM WOMEN EMPOWERMENT BAJOH, CAMEROON  

2015 SUMMER

In Bajoh, a village in Cameroon, the women had to walk 3 miles each way to a waterfall to collect water for their families each day.  This led to many girls dropping out of school because they had to collect water for the village.  We provided financing and worked with a Peace Corps volunteer to create a pipeline to bring the water from the waterfall directly to three villages to avoid the need for the women to walk through the forest to the waterfall.  Later, water.org requested us to let them adopt the project and expanded it to include bringing water to two other villages.  Since this freed up more time for the women of the village, we also gave goats to the village to allow women to raise them to provide fresh milk for their families.  In addition, we have created a microloan fund to provide funding for different business ventures for groups of women in the village.  This has created chicken farms, agriculture, beehives, soap production, and snail farms within the village.
